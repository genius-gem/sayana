# SAYANA PRESS KNOWLEDGE BASE

## GENERAL INFORMATION

### What is Sayana Press?

Sayana Press is a contraceptive injection used to prevent pregnancy. It contains depot medroxyprogesterone acetate (DMPA) and is administered as a subcutaneous injection.

### How does Sayana Press work?

Sayana Press prevents pregnancy by:

* Preventing ovulation.
* Thickening cervical mucus to block sperm.
* Thinning the lining of the uterus.

### How effective is Sayana Press?

When used correctly and on schedule every 13 weeks, Sayana Press is more than 99% effective in preventing pregnancy.

### How often is Sayana Press administered?

One injection is required every 13 weeks (3 months).

### Is Sayana Press the same as Depo-Provera?

Both contain DMPA, but Sayana Press:

* Uses a smaller needle.
* Is injected under the skin.
* Can be self-administered after proper training.

---

# USE AND ADMINISTRATION

## Injection Sites

Sayana Press can be injected into:

* Thigh
* Abdomen
* Upper arm

## Self-Injection

Many women can safely self-inject after receiving training from a healthcare professional.

### Is self-injection safe?

Yes. Research shows self-injection is safe and effective when women are properly trained.

### Incorrect Self-Injection

If you are unsure whether you injected correctly, contact a healthcare provider immediately.

### Storage

Store Sayana Press:

* At room temperature
* Away from direct sunlight
* Out of reach of children

### Pain During Injection

Most women report little or mild pain because the needle is small.

---

# ELIGIBILITY

## Who Can Use Sayana Press?

Most women can use Sayana Press, including:

* Women who have children
* Women seeking birth spacing
* Breastfeeding mothers after six weeks postpartum
* Eligible adolescents

## Who Should Not Use Sayana Press?

Women with:

* Unexplained vaginal bleeding
* Breast cancer
* Severe liver disease
* Allergy to DMPA

A healthcare provider should determine suitability.

---

# BREASTFEEDING

## Can breastfeeding mothers use Sayana Press?

Yes. Sayana Press is generally considered safe for breastfeeding mothers after six weeks postpartum.

# SIDE EFFECTS

## Common Side Effects

Common side effects include:

* Irregular menstrual bleeding
* Amenorrhea (absence of monthly periods)
* Weight gain
* Headache
* Mood changes
* Breast tenderness
* Dizziness
* Fatigue
* Mild abdominal discomfort
* Reduced sexual desire

Most side effects improve over time and are not dangerous.

---

## Irregular Menstrual Bleeding

### Home Care

* Use sanitary pads if needed.
* Keep a menstrual diary.
* Stay hydrated.
* Eat iron-rich foods.
* Rest when tired.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Bleeding becomes very heavy.
* Dizziness develops.
* Fainting occurs.
* Bleeding lasts many weeks.

---

## Amenorrhea (No Monthly Period)

### Home Care

* Understand that this is common.
* Continue injections on schedule.
* Track injection dates carefully.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Pregnancy is suspected.
* Severe abdominal pain occurs.

---

## Weight Gain

### Home Care

* Reduce sugary foods and drinks.
* Exercise regularly.
* Maintain a balanced diet.
* Monitor weight monthly.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Weight gain becomes excessive or rapid.

---

## Injection Site Reactions

### Home Care

* Apply a cold compress.
* Keep the area clean.
* Avoid scratching.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Redness worsens.
* Pus develops.
* Fever occurs.
* Severe pain develops.

---

## Headache

### Home Care

* Rest.
* Drink water.
* Sleep adequately.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Severe headache occurs.
* Blurred vision develops.
* Confusion occurs.

---

## Mood Changes

### Home Care

* Talk with trusted family or friends.
* Exercise regularly.
* Engage in enjoyable activities.
* Manage stress.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Depression becomes severe.
* Persistent sadness occurs.
* Thoughts of self-harm develop.

---

## Breast Tenderness

### Home Care

* Wear supportive bras.
* Reduce caffeine intake.
* Use warm or cold compresses.

### Seek Medical Help If

* A breast lump develops.
* Severe breast pain occurs.
* Nipple discharge occurs.

---

## Dizziness

### Home Care

* Drink fluids.
* Eat nutritious meals.
* Rest adequately.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Persistent dizziness occurs.
* Fainting occurs.

---

## Fatigue

### Home Care

* Sleep adequately.
* Maintain good nutrition.
* Stay hydrated.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Fatigue becomes severe or prolonged.

---

## Abdominal Discomfort

### Home Care

* Rest.
* Stay hydrated.
* Apply a warm compress.

### Seek Medical Help If

* Severe abdominal pain develops.

---

## Reduced Sexual Desire

### Home Care

* Communicate openly with your partner.
* Reduce stress.
* Seek emotional support.

### Seek Medical Help If

* The issue causes significant distress.

# PREGNANCY AND FERTILITY

## Can Sayana Press Cause Infertility?

No.

Fertility usually returns after discontinuing Sayana Press, although return to fertility may take several months.

## Can Pregnancy Occur While Using Sayana Press?

Pregnancy is rare when injections are received correctly and on time.

## Pregnancy During Use

If pregnancy occurs or is suspected, consult a healthcare provider.

## Accidental Use During Pregnancy

Current evidence does not indicate major harm to the baby, but medical consultation is advised.

## Delayed Return of Fertility

Some women may take several months before fertility returns after stopping Sayana Press.

---

# MISSED INJECTIONS

## What Should I Do If I Miss My Injection Date?

Visit a healthcare facility as soon as possible.

### If Less Than Four Weeks Late

You may usually continue with the injection.

### If Significantly Late

Pregnancy may need to be ruled out before another injection is given.

## Preventing Missed Injections

Use:

* Phone alarms
* Reminder apps
* Calendar reminders
* Appointment cards

---

# SEXUALLY TRANSMITTED INFECTIONS

## Does Sayana Press Protect Against STIs?

No.

Sayana Press prevents pregnancy but does not protect against:

* HIV
* Gonorrhea
* Chlamydia
* Syphilis
* Other sexually transmitted infections

Condom use is recommended for STI protection.

---

# MYTHS AND FACTS

## Myth: Sayana Press causes permanent infertility.

Fact: Fertility usually returns after discontinuation.

## Myth: Stopping periods means pregnancy.

Fact: Amenorrhea is a common effect of Sayana Press.

## Myth: Sayana Press moves around inside the body.

Fact: The medicine remains under the skin and is gradually absorbed.

## Myth: Sayana Press permanently damages the womb.

Fact: No scientific evidence supports this claim.

## Myth: Sayana Press causes birth defects later.

Fact: There is no evidence that prior use causes birth defects.

## Myth: Sayana Press is only for married women.

Fact: Any medically eligible woman may use it.

# EMERGENCY WARNING SIGNS

Seek immediate medical attention if any of the following occur:

* Heavy uncontrolled bleeding
* Severe chest pain
* Difficulty breathing
* Severe headache with vision changes
* Severe abdominal pain
* High fever
* Signs of allergic reaction
* Fainting

These symptoms require urgent medical evaluation.

---

# HEALTH EDUCATION MESSAGE

Most side effects of Sayana Press are mild and improve over time. Keeping injection appointments, maintaining healthy habits, and seeking medical advice when severe symptoms occur help ensure safe and effective use.
